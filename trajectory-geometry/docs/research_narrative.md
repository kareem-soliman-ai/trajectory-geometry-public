# Trajectory Geometry: Research Narrative

**Status:** Current narrative companion to the full research history  
**Companion:** [`research-history.md`](./research-history.md)

## The Arc In One Line

This programme began as a search for static semantic shape and became a study of computation as a dynamical process.

## Phase I: Looking For Shapes

The earliest experiments asked whether cognitive operations left identifiable signatures in output embeddings or hidden-state summaries. Those attempts mostly failed. Topic noise dominated, scalar summaries threw away directionality, and the original Dynamic Semantic Geometry framing turned out to be too crude.

That failure mattered. It forced the project away from static representations and toward temporal structure.

## Phase II: From Snapshots To Motion

The turning point was the realization that reasoning should not be studied as a static state but as a trajectory. Once hidden states were treated as paths through latent space, differential-geometric and dynamical metrics began to separate regimes that static probing could not.

This was the real birth of Trajectory Geometry.

## Phase III: Failure Becomes Structured

The next breakthrough was not just that successful and failed runs differ, but that failure itself has structure.

- Some failures collapse early into a thin, low-dimensional path.
- Others explore broadly but never converge.

This made it possible to talk about computational failure modes mechanistically rather than behaviourally.

## Phase IV: Success Is Not One Thing

The research then overturned a tempting but wrong idea: there is no universal success geometry.

Direct-answer success and chain-of-thought success are not just different in degree; they are often different in sign. Metrics that indicate success in one regime can indicate failure in another. The right frame became regime-relativity rather than a global "good trajectory."

That result substantially sharpened the project's scientific credibility because it replaced a simplistic universal story with a more constrained and falsifiable one.

## Phase V: Robustness And Invariance

Once the key signatures replicated across scale and across architecture families, the programme shifted from discovery to robustness.

This is where the major invariant claims emerged:

- the Success Attractor
- architecture-invariant metric signatures
- the Goldilocks interpretation of controlled expansion
- PCR-based recovery of within-regime quality signal

At this stage, the project stopped looking like a one-model curiosity and started looking like a real empirical programme.

## Phase VI: Domain Transfer And Caution

EXP-20 was important because it showed what not to overclaim. Trajectory geometry did detect differences in empathy-related tasks, but factorial analysis showed that much of the signal belonged to prose framing rather than the intended construct.

That refutation strengthened the programme. It demonstrated that the method could fail in an interpretable way and that construct validity would need careful controls outside arithmetic.

## Phase VII: Mechanistic Deepening

With Gemma replication and SAE analysis, the project moved closer to mechanistic interpretability. The big shift here was from asking whether geometric signatures exist to asking whether they can be tied to causal substrates in feature space.

EXP-22 provided the first proof-of-concept that the answer is yes. Some selected SAE features could be ablated with selective disruption of carry-requiring arithmetic, which begins to close the loop from behaviour to geometry to mechanism.

## Phase VIII: The Dynamic Functional Architecture

EXP-23 (Perturbation Spectroscopy) demonstrated that these causal structures are not static modules. The discovery of **Infrastructure Bottlenecks** (high-frequency nodes like Feature 869) and the "Causal Decoupling" of Feature 6340 proved that as problem complexity scales (e.g., from simple arithmetic to the "Cognitive Decathlon"), Gemma 3 1B dynamically redistributes computation. It abandons narrow arithmetic subspaces in favor of more robust, generalized logic circuitry (Dynamic Circuit Reconfiguration).

## Where The Programme Is Now

The research now sits at the frontier of dynamic interpretability:
- a mature observational framework for analysing computational trajectories
- an emerging causal framework for validating how those trajectories correspond to genuine, dynamically-allocated mechanisms

The current phase (Tier-2 Spectroscopy, PS-4) has successfully geometrically profiled these nodes. By comparing the trajectory corruption of infrastructure bottlenecks (869) against specialized logic gates (212), we can now distinguish between global structural collapse and targeted logical erasure.
